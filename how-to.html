Simple Spreads to get you started

The Question

All spreads start with the same thing, a question. The point of tarot reading isn't just to see the future as many may believe, it is the art of answering a question brought to the reader. With a question in mind you can ask the cards and begin your spread. Questions can be as simple as "will I get a promotion?" to more complex querires about about Love and Family. With a question asked, have your subject pick the cards in order and arrange these in a spread. Different spreads will be better suited to different queries and it's important to consider what arrangement will be best before you start.
Below we've selected 3 simple spreads to start your interprting journey, remember that a card in an inverted position (upside down) actually has a different meaning and always respect your deck!

Simple Three Card Spread

Spread Summary

This is the most basic spread and is a good starting point if you've just begun your Tarot journey. This layout is good at suggesting some sort of linear path, sequence of events or cause and effect.

Position Meaning

  1. Past/Situation
  2. Present/Action
  3. Future/Outcome
Crossed Three Card Spread

Spread Summary

As the layout would suggest, it's about understanding conflicts and obstacles. The card that crosses over is something that stands in the way and that needs to be overcome, something that blocks you from attaining your goal, while the third card acts as a sort of advice card, looking at the situation from a more distant viewpoint.

Position Meaning

  1. Situation/Issue
  2. Challenge/Ways to Overcome
  3. Advice/Goal
Common Celtic Cross

Spread Summary

This is spread most commonly used by Tarot readers and great for answering complex questions. It can be hard to interpret due to the all the dynamics between the cards but once mastered it's always a good spread to reach for. It's important to think about the relationships between each card in this spread, do the Outcome and Above match, how do the Hope/Fears interact with the Below etc. Remember not to just read the cards one-by-one and think of the whole spread as a story.

Position Meaning

  1. The Present
    Reflective of the subject now
  2. The Challenge
    Even positive cards drawn here can be a challenge
  3. The Past
    Represents events leading to the present situation
  4. The Future
    What is likely to occur soon but not the outcome
  5. Above
    This represents the subjects personal idea of the best outcome
  6. Below
    A card reflecting the person's underlying feelings towards the situation
  7. Advice
    This position takes into account everything and recomends what approach to take
  8. External Influences
    This card represents people or events outside the subjects control which will affect the situation
  9. Hopes and/or Fears
    The subjects hopes and or fears about the situations outcome
  10. The Outcome
    This is based on the person continuing their actions without change
